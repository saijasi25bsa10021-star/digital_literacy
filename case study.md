With the rise of digital payments in India, UPI fraud has become very common. In a typical case, a college student receives a call from someone pretending to be a bank representative. The caller claims there is an issue with the student’s account and asks them to verify details.

The fraudster then sends a payment request via a UPI app and instructs the student to “approve” it to receive money. Confused, the student enters their UPI PIN, unknowingly authorizing a debit transaction instead of a credit. Within seconds, money is deducted from their account.

Such scams often target students and elderly individuals who may not fully understand how UPI transactions work. Fraudsters create urgency and panic to prevent victims from thinking clearly.

The consequences include financial loss, emotional stress, and sometimes difficulty recovering the money. In many cases, victims hesitate to report the incident due to embarrassment or lack of awareness.

This type of fraud highlights the importance of digital awareness and cautious behavior while using online payment systems.
